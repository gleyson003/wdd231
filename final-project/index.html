The state of Pará is preparing to host the 30th UN Conference on Climate Change (COP30), to be held in Belém (PA), in November 2025. According to estimates by the Getúlio Vargas Foundation (FGV), a flow of of more than 40 thousand visitors during the main days of the Conference. Of this total, approximately 7,000 make up the so-called "COP family", formed by UN teams and delegations from member countries.

COP30 is the 30th United Nations Climate Change Conference (Conference of the Parties), an annual global meeting where world leaders, scientists, non-governmental organizations and civil society representatives discuss actions to combat climate change. It is considered one of the main events on the subject in the world.

About Belém

The city of Belém is the capital of the northern state of Pará. The municipality is known throughout Brazil for the richness of its musical genres and the diversity of its cuisine.

Belém is the capital and most populous city in the state of Pará. It is located in Guajará Bay, in the northern portion of Pará territory, in an area characterized by high humidity. The city was founded by Portuguese colonizers, who, given its local geostrategic importance, built military installations in Belém territory. Belém's great economic boom occurred during the rubber cycle, in which the city played an important role as a commercial warehouse. Currently, the local economy is anchored in the tertiary sector. The city's government is structured into a division of the Three Powers.

Belém was founded in 1616, following the construction of Forte do Presépio by the Portuguese forces that occupied the region. Belemense's climate is Equatorial. The city's climatic characteristics include high temperatures, large amounts of rainfall and high humidity. The municipality of Belém has around 1.5 million inhabitants. It is the most populous in Pará. In the Brazilian context, it is the 11th in population. The city also has some large industries as Coca-Cola company. The city has 71 official neighborhoods.
